FALCON 9

Engineering a masterpiece for the future

The Falcon 9 is truly a marvel of engineering. Developed and manufactured by SpaceX, Falcon 9 is capable of both cargo and crew missions as well as vertical landings.

Falcon 9 is currently the most affordable way to put satellites or humans into orbit.

Payload

Adaptive and modular

Atop the Falcon 9 rocket sits the payload which can carry both crew and cargo.

By changing the configuration of the rocket, SpaceX can maximize cargo capacity for unmanned flights.

The Crew Dragon was successfully launched with a Falcon 9 rocket and brought two astronauts to the International Space Station in May of 2020.

Second stage

Orbital precision

The second stage engine takes over after stage separation and is in charge of bringing the payload to the desired orbit.

Designed for multiple ignitions, the second stage engine can be used to place several payloads into different orbits.

Interstage

Stage separation

The interstage connects the first and second stage of the Falcon 9 rocket. It is in charge of the release and separation system that decouples the two stages after Falcon 9 leaves the atmosphere.

The grid fins that help guide the first stage back to a safe and vertical landing are also attached to the interstage.

First stage

The heavy lift

The first stage takes Falcon 9 and its payload from the launch pad and through the atmosphere, using its nine Merlin engines to escape earths gravity.

Upon decoupling, the first stage returns to earth, using its grid fins to guide it down to the launch pad or drone ship. The entry burn slows its descent, allowing it to land softly and be reused.
